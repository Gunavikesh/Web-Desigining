What is Graphology?

In a simple definition Graphology is a projective technique that deals with understanding human psychology, evaluating personality traits of an individual based on the study of patterns and characteristics of one’s handwriting. It is also considered as a pseudo-science as it has been evolved based on various scientific methods and techniques. Apart from handwriting, Graphology also deals with the study of Signatures and Doodles as well.

Myths about Graphology

  • Graphology can reveal about age and sex accurately.
  • Graphology can tell about physical illness
  • Graphology can predict the past and future

Positive writing and Negative writing

With respect to Graphology, any handwriting falls into the category of either positive or negative based on the personality trait they refer, depending on the formation of the letters and patterns in the writing.

Usually Positive writing is clear and generous, whereas negative writing appears as narrow in its flow and messy.

Signature

Signature is a key indicator of your identity. It speaks about how do you want to be perceived by the world.

The formation of the signature, alignment, placement, content and size of the signature is considered to carry out the handwriting analysis.

Basics of Graphology:

Zones:

The vertical divisions created for any handwriting are known as Zones. There are 3 zones that are considered in graphology i.e. Upper, Middle and Lower. The upper zone (superior zone) refers to spirituality, intelligence; middle zone for daily based activities and lower zone (inferior) refers to materialistic features such as money, sexuality etc. The level dominance of these three zones in the writing decides the overall personality of a person.

Margins:

Margin is the space left between the handwriting and the edge of the paper. In graphology left and right margins are considered for the handwriting analysis. The size of the left and right margin talks about the depth of the person’s attachment towards its past and future. In another way, it refers to how the individual is related to its environment and its reality.

Slant

Slant in a handwriting is the direction or the tilt of the letters. Handwriting slant is considered to understand the emotional level in an individual. From left most inclination to right most slant represents the most emotional approach to the most logical or practical approach.

Pressure

The pressure in handwriting refers to the force of the pen on the paper during the writing activity. The pressure from the pen results due to the psychic pressure that the person’s brain would be experiencing at that time. This parameter talks about the emotional intensity of the person at that point of time.

Baseline

Baseline in handwriting in an imaginary reference horizontal line around which the letters are arranged. The direction and frame of the baseline reveal about the outlook of the person towards life in terms of optimism, hopefulness, enthusiasm and so on.

Size

In handwriting analysis, a relative size measurement is taken into consideration. The average body of the letters is of range 1.5mm to 2.5mm, is considered as small size, whereas the size between 2.5mm to 3.5mm is marked as middle size and size greater than 3.5mm is larger size. The size is analyzed to find out about how a person thinks of self or value to self.

Spacing

In graphology spacing is mentioned as spacing between words and spacing between letters. Spacing between words refers the extent of socialization in one’s personality, whereas spacing between letters reveal how much individual is self-expressive.

Connection

The connection in graphology refers to the connection between the letters. There are variable connections that are being observed in handwriting's based on the letter, writing styling. The connection between the letters talks about the thoughts and feelings of the person towards another person. In return, it reveals about the behavior patterns of the one towards others.

Pastocity:

Pastocity in a handwriting refers to the flow of additional ink from the paper on the paper. Usually it reveals about nature of your interest or liking such as liking about food, movies, dressing, music etc. based on the thickness of the ink on the paper. Based on the impact of the interest pastocity is divided as 2 types 1. Positive 2. Negative

Doodling:

Doodling is the act of drawing random scrawls on papers without being much attentive towards any particular theme or topic. In graphology doodling is considered one of the important handwriting specimens. Doodling are counted as related to state of subconscious mind of the individual, and hence they provide much deeper clues about someone’s behavior, beliefs and habits.

Capital letters:

Apart from grammatical rules, capital letters are usually observed as one adopt as per his/her common sense to represent something with a virtual shout for attention. From psychological perspective, usage of capital letters connects to Ego level of an individual. In other words, it talks about how someone perceives about self, possess the level of aspiration.

Ink color

The ink color that a user chooses to use for his or her writing is also result of state of mind. It speaks about the emotional attitude of the writer at that point. The pattern in choosing the color of the ink also matters from the perspective of knowing about personality traits.

Punctuation marks:

Punctuation marks such as commas, apostrophes, quotations, periods, and question mark etc are important elements in grammar. But usually when a writer writes being personal, one tends to use punctuation marks based on own interpretation of the thoughts. Depending on the amount of usage, positions of usage in the writing do relates to one’s personality and state of mind in graphology field.
